## Historical Analysis: How the Opinion Became "Necessity"

### The Crystallization Process

#### **1. Ancient Origins** (Greek Mathematics)
- **Context**: Geometric idealism, eternal forms, perfect reasoning
- **Goal**: Eliminate the messy, approximate, contradictory aspects of experience
- **Method**: Focus on clear, distinct, decidable mathematical objects
- **Result**: Logic becomes associated with "perfect" reasoning

#### **2. Medieval Systematization** (Scholastic Logic)
- **Context**: Theological systematization, Aristotelian synthesis
- **Goal**: Create complete, consistent doctrinal systems
- **Method**: Formalize logical rules to ensure systematic consistency
- **Result**: Contradiction becomes associated with heresy/error

#### **3. Modern Formalization** (19th-20th Century)
- **Context**: Mathematical rigor, scientific method, mechanical computation
- **Goal**: Make logic completely precise and mechanical
- **Method**: Axiomatize logical systems with strict formal rules
- **Result**: Classical logic becomes identified with "logical rigor"

#### **4. Academic Institutionalization** (20th Century)
- **Context**: University departments, textbook standardization, pedagogical systematization
- **Goal**: Teach logic as foundational skill for all rational disciplines
- **Method**: Present classical logic as "how logic works" rather than "one approach to logic"
- **Result**: Classical logic becomes culturally invisible as a particular choice
