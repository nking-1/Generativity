## The Traditional Paradigms

### Discovery Paradigm
**Core belief**: Truths exist independently in an abstract realm, waiting to be discovered through rational investigation.

**Implications**:
- Mathematical objects have eternal, objective existence
- Scientific laws are "out there" in nature's structure
- Human minds access pre-existing truth through reasoning
- Truth is binary: statements are definitely true or false
- The goal is to uncover what was always already true

### Invention Paradigm  
**Core belief**: Truths are human constructions, created through cultural and cognitive processes.

**Implications**:
- Mathematical objects are mental/linguistic constructions
- Scientific theories are useful models we create
- Human minds construct truth through social interaction
- Truth is relative: depends on frameworks and contexts
- The goal is to build useful conceptual systems

### The Inadequacy of Both
**Discovery paradigm problems**: Where is the abstract realm? How do minds access it? Why does it match physical reality?

**Invention paradigm problems**: Why do different minds converge? Why is mathematics so effective? Why do constructions constrain their creators?
