# Computational Reality: DAO Framework vs. Existing Approaches

## Overview

The relationship between computation and reality has been explored through various theoretical lenses, from digital physics to pancomputationalism. The DAO framework presents a novel approach that differs fundamentally from existing computational theories of reality. This note examines these differences and assesses what has been constructively established as a coherent metaphysical position.

## Major Approaches to Computational Reality

### 1. Digital Physics / "It from Bit" (Wheeler, Zuse, Lloyd)

**Core claim**: Physical reality emerges from information processing; bits are more fundamental than particles.

**Approach**: Reality consists of discrete computational units (bits, qubits, cellular automata) that process information according to physical laws.

**Key question**: "How do information processing operations give rise to physical phenomena?"

### 2. Simulation Hypothesis (Bostrom, others)

**Core claim**: Our reality might be a computational simulation running on some external computational substrate.

**Approach**: Assumes external computational hardware and programmers; we are patterns within their simulation.

**Key question**: "Are we computational entities inside someone else's computer?"

### 3. Cellular Automata Universe (Wolfram, Fredkin)

**Core claim**: Reality follows simple computational rules that iterate to produce complex behavior.

**Approach**: The universe operates like a vast cellular automaton with simple local rules generating all observed complexity.

**Key question**: "What simple computational rules could generate our observed reality?"

### 4. Computational Theory of Mind (CTM)

**Core claim**: Mental processes are computational processes; consciousness emerges from sufficiently complex computation.

**Approach**: Brains are biological computers; thoughts are computational states and transitions.

**Key question**: "How does computation in neural networks give rise to consciousness?"

### 5. Integrated Information Theory (IIT)

**Core claim**: Consciousness corresponds to integrated information (Φ) in a system.

**Approach**: Quantifies consciousness through mathematical measures of information integration.

**Key question**: "How much integrated information does a system contain?"

### 6. Pancomputationalism (Zuse, Lloyd, others)

**Core claim**: All physical processes are computational processes; everything computes.

**Approach**: Even rocks, planets, and fundamental particles perform computations as part of their physical behavior.

**Key question**: "What computations are being performed by different physical systems?"
